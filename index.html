Learn the Cube

Understand the cube’s structure: centers never move, edges have two colors, corners have three. This makes solving a matter of placing the right piece in the right spot with the right orientation.

Intuition First

Start with beginner’s layer-by-layer. See how moves relocate pieces without breaking what you’ve solved.

Pattern Recognition

Learn common algorithms that solve repeatable patterns: cross, F2L pairs, OLL, and PLL.

Speed Fundamentals

Learn lookahead, finger tricks, and turning efficiency to drop your times without learning hundreds of algs.

Solving Methods

Pick a method that matches your goals. You can always switch or blend techniques later.

CFOP

Cross • F2L • OLL • PLL. The most popular speedcubing method with abundant resources and alg sets.

Typical: Fast with practice • Many algs for full OLL/PLL

Roux

Block building and M-slice efficiency. Great ergonomics and few rotations once mastered.

Typical: Fewer algs • High TPS potential

ZZ

EOLine first to orient edges, then rotationless F2L. Smooth and ergonomic turning.

Typical: Smart inspection • Intuitive F2L

Beginner Steps (Layer-by-Layer)

  1. Step 1
    White Cross

    Match white edges to their center colors to form a plus sign.

  2. Step 2
    White Corners

    Insert corners to complete the first layer.

  3. Step 3
    Second Layer Edges

    Place edges using left/right insertion triggers.

  4. Step 4
    Yellow Cross

    Orient top edges to make a cross (F R U R' U' F').

  1. Step 5
    Orient Yellow Corners

    Use corner orientation algs to face all yellows up.

  2. Step 6
    Position Yellow Corners

    Swap corners to the correct locations (e.g., U R U' L' U R' U' L).

  3. Step 7
    Position Yellow Edges

    Cycle edges to finish the cube (e.g., the U-perm).

  4. Tip
    Notation

    R, L, U, D, F, B with ' for counter-clockwise and 2 for double turns.

FAQ

How long does it take to learn? +

Most learners solve their first cube in a day or two. With practice, sub-1 minute is common in a week or two.

Do I need to memorize many algorithms? +

Not at first. Beginner LBL uses a handful. As you get faster, you can add OLL/PLL gradually.

What cube should I buy? +

Any modern 3x3 speedcube (magnetic if possible). Popular: MoYu RS3M, GAN 356, QiYi Tornado.

How do I get faster? +

Turn smoothly, look ahead into the next pair, reduce rotations, and practice consistent triggers.
